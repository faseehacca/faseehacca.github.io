*Financial Reporting (FR) · Skills Level*

---
## Core Principle

Inventories are valued at the **lower of cost and net realisable value (NRV).**

---
## Cost

Cost includes all costs incurred in bringing the inventories to their **present location and condition.**

| Component            | Includes                                                                                                                                                                   |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Purchase Costs**   | Purchase price, import duties, non-refundable taxes, transport/handling *Less:* trade discounts & rebates                                                                  |
| **Conversion Costs** | Direct labour, direct expenses, production overhead (POH) at normal level of activity                                                                                      |
| **Other Costs**      | NPOH only if incurred in bringing inventories to present location and condition. Borrowing costs in limited circumstances. Joint product costs (deduct NRV of by-products) |

---
## Excluded Costs

The following costs are **excluded** and charged as expenses:

- Abnormal waste
- Storage costs (unless necessary to the production process)
- Administrative overhead that does not contribute to bringing inventories to their present location and condition
- Selling costs

---

## Net Realisable Value (NRV)

> NRV = Selling Price − Cost to Completion − Selling Costs

---
**Carriage Inwards** — cost incurred by the **buyer** when purchasing goods and bringing them to its own premises. *(Included in cost of inventory)*

**Carriage Outwards** — cost incurred by the **seller** when transporting goods to customers. *(Excluded — selling cost)*
